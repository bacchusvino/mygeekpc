Remote Diagnostic Services – Client Consent & Responsibility Agreement

Please read the following terms carefully before proceeding.

1. Purpose of This Service

This remote diagnostic service is designed to identify hardware, software, configuration, and performance issues on your computer system.

It is diagnostic in nature, not a repair, data recovery, or backup service unless explicitly agreed to in writing.

2. Scope of Work (What This Is)

By approving this service, you understand that the technician will:

  • Collect system information (hardware, OS version, storage health, logs, running services)
  • Analyze the data to identify likely causes of problems
  • Provide a written summary of findings and recommended next steps

No changes, repairs, removals, updates, or optimizations will be performed without your explicit approval.

3. What This Service Is Not

Unless separately contracted, this service does not include:

  • Data backup or data recovery
  • File-level review of personal documents
  • Malware removal or system cleaning
  • Operating system reinstallation
  • Hardware replacement
  • Guaranteed resolution of all issues

Diagnostics identify problems; they do not magically fix them. (Sadly.)

4. Backup Responsibility (Very Important)

You acknowledge and agree that:

  • You are solely responsible for ensuring your data is backed up before diagnostics begin.
  • Remote diagnostics may reveal existing issues (disk failure, corruption, malware, misconfiguration) that already threaten your data.
  • The technician is not responsible for data loss, corruption, or system failure resulting from:
    • Pre-existing conditions
    • Hardware failure
    • Software defects
    • Operating system instability
    • Prior malware or encryption damage
Optional Backup Service

If you want assistance backing up your system:
  • Backup services must be requested separately
  • Backup services may require an on-site visit
  • Backup services are billed separately and are not included in remote diagnostics
No backup requested = no backup performed.

5. Known Risks & "Horror Stories" (Transparency Section)

You understand that computers sometimes fail during or shortly after diagnostics due to issues such as:

  • Drives failing under read stress (especially aging HDDs/SSDs)
  • Systems crashing due to thermal, memory, or power issues
  • Operating systems revealing corruption when logs are accessed
  • Malware or ransomware activating when disturbed
  • Previously hidden hardware faults surfacing

These risks exist regardless of who is diagnosing the system.

Diagnostics do not cause these problems — they expose them.

6. Remote Access & Security Consent

By approving this service, you consent to:

  • Temporary remote access using secure tools (e.g., encrypted SSH or equivalent)
  • Automated scripts collecting system-level information only
  • No intentional access to personal documents, photos, emails, or browser content

Access can be revoked by you at any time.

7. AI-Assisted Analysis Disclosure

You acknowledge that:

  • Diagnostic results may be reviewed using AI-assisted analysis tools
  • AI is used to summarize logs, identify patterns, and suggest likely causes
  • Final decisions, recommendations, and actions remain human-reviewed

AI does not make changes to your system.

8. No Guarantee Clause

You understand and agree that:

  • Diagnostics do not guarantee problem resolution
  • Some issues may require on-site service or hardware replacement
  • Some systems may be beyond economical repair

You are paying for expert analysis, not a promise of resurrection.

9. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law:

  • The technician is not liable for data loss, downtime, or consequential damages
  • Liability is limited to the amount paid for the diagnostic service itself
